## panel-refresh-2026q2 (2026-05-20)
- **Technique selection:** Default vertical slicing was correct for this domain — all 5 candidates had clear code-layer boundaries (`styles.py`, `appearance_panel.py`, `app.py`, `surfaces.py`) and no journey-shaped or bounded-context-unclear shape. The brief's explicit first-wave bundle ordering (UPL-3 → UPL-1 → UPL-5 → UPL-19+18 spike → UPL-13) mapped directly to 5 epics without any alternative cut that deserved a gate.
- **Epic sizing heuristics:** XS candidates (UPL-3, UPL-13) were promoted to their own epics rather than bundled, because (a) they have distinct predecessor relationships (UPL-3 feeds e4 via render-pipeline stability; UPL-13 is truly independent) and (b) each covers a different vertical slice of `app.py`. A "launch quick wins" bundle would have obscured the DAG. UPL-13 sits at e5 with no predecessors — sequenced last per brief but coded as independent to let Phase 3 lane it freely.
- **Specialist hint patterns:** AI-9 (re-entrancy) and AI-10 (raw mesh cache) appeared as hints in 4 of 5 epics — every epic touching `app.py:_render_current` needs both. AI-13 (6-digit hex) appeared in 3 of 5 epics wherever hex flows into PyVista or `styles.py`. AI-6 (implicit vs parametric pipeline) was critical only for e4 (Enriques generator — must not skip Taubin or switch pipelines). AI-8 (Surface/ParamSpec frozen dataclass) was a load-bearing spike concern for e4.
- **ENABLER ratio:** 1/5 = 20% enabler epics (e2 = UPL-1 palette tokens). The downstream value epic (e3) was always named in e2's body, satisfying the hard rule that pure-enabler chains must have explicit downstream value.
- **Spike handling:** UPL-18's mandatory render-time spike was embedded inside e4's acceptance signals (not as a separate epic) because the spike is a go/no-go gate on a single implementation decision, not a standalone deliverable. Embedding the spike timing log as an acceptance signal (`/tmp/enriques-taubin-spike.txt`) keeps the epic count down and makes the spike outcome auditable.

## realtime-variety-render (2026-05-22)
- **Technique selection:** Default vertical slicing was correct. The 13-candidate set had clear code-layer boundaries (`app.py` render pipeline, `surfaces.py` generators, optional `ui_helpers.py`) and the brief's DAG mapped cleanly to 6 epics without any alternative cut deserving a gate. The one non-default decision was treating the CAND-4 macOS spike as its own `[ENABLER]` epic (e3) rather than embedding it inside a larger value epic — justified because the spike's go/no-go outcome gates two downstream epics (e4, e5) and without it those epics are Estimable=unknown.
- **Epic sizing heuristics:** Bundling the five XS/S quick-wins (CAND-12, CAND-13, CAND-5, CAND-6, CAND-11) into a single e1 was the right call — each is NONE-challenger, they share the same `app.py`/`surfaces.py` slice, and separating them would inflate the epic count to 10+ with artificially thin INVEST profiles. Conversely, CAND-4 + CAND-3 were combined into one L epic (e4) because CAND-3 without CAND-4 produces 55ms-blocking stutter — not "continuous drag" — so the challenger's "strict, not soft" dependency made them a single vertical slice. CAND-2 (Numba) stayed separate as e5 (M) because it has a different specialist surface (`@njit` kernels + numerical tests) and clear v0/v1 scoping.
- **Specialist hint patterns:** AI-9 (re-entrancy) appeared in e1 (CAND-5 queue-latest) and e4 (worker replaces `processEvents` workaround) — every epic touching `_render_current` or the `_computing` guard needs it. AI-15 (math honesty / disclaimer) appeared in e4 (coarse-preview badge timing) and e5 (Numba transcription risk) — the AI-15 concern bifurcates across the LOD and JIT epics in this domain. AI-6 (implicit vs parametric pipeline) appeared in e2 (Hanson fast-path must not enable coarse-LOD), e4 (coarse-path guard for Hanson), and e6 (Flying Edges retains post-step normals) — three separate epics touching the implicit/parametric split.
- **ENABLER ratio:** 1/6 = 17% ENABLER (e3 spike). Acceptable; e3's downstream value epics (e4, e5) are named in its body and its specialist hints cross-reference the spike artifacts.
- **Dependency graph shape:** The DAG has two parallel tracks after e1 — e2 (Hanson fast-path, S, independent of the spike) and e3→e4→e5 (architectural arc). e6 (Flying Edges) hangs off e1 as a fully independent S track. This "two parallel tracks + one independent" shape is the natural output when a roadmap has both a quick-win "free" variety-family path and a mandatory spike-gated architectural path; name both tracks explicitly in the DAG table rather than serialising everything.
