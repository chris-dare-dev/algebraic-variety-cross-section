## panel-refresh-2026q2 (2026-05-20)
- **Technique selection:** Default vertical slicing was correct for this domain — all 5 candidates had clear code-layer boundaries (`styles.py`, `appearance_panel.py`, `app.py`, `surfaces.py`) and no journey-shaped or bounded-context-unclear shape. The brief's explicit first-wave bundle ordering (UPL-3 → UPL-1 → UPL-5 → UPL-19+18 spike → UPL-13) mapped directly to 5 epics without any alternative cut that deserved a gate.
- **Epic sizing heuristics:** XS candidates (UPL-3, UPL-13) were promoted to their own epics rather than bundled, because (a) they have distinct predecessor relationships (UPL-3 feeds e4 via render-pipeline stability; UPL-13 is truly independent) and (b) each covers a different vertical slice of `app.py`. A "launch quick wins" bundle would have obscured the DAG. UPL-13 sits at e5 with no predecessors — sequenced last per brief but coded as independent to let Phase 3 lane it freely.
- **Specialist hint patterns:** AI-9 (re-entrancy) and AI-10 (raw mesh cache) appeared as hints in 4 of 5 epics — every epic touching `app.py:_render_current` needs both. AI-13 (6-digit hex) appeared in 3 of 5 epics wherever hex flows into PyVista or `styles.py`. AI-6 (implicit vs parametric pipeline) was critical only for e4 (Enriques generator — must not skip Taubin or switch pipelines). AI-8 (Surface/ParamSpec frozen dataclass) was a load-bearing spike concern for e4.
- **ENABLER ratio:** 1/5 = 20% enabler epics (e2 = UPL-1 palette tokens). The downstream value epic (e3) was always named in e2's body, satisfying the hard rule that pure-enabler chains must have explicit downstream value.
- **Spike handling:** UPL-18's mandatory render-time spike was embedded inside e4's acceptance signals (not as a separate epic) because the spike is a go/no-go gate on a single implementation decision, not a standalone deliverable. Embedding the spike timing log as an acceptance signal (`/tmp/enriques-taubin-spike.txt`) keeps the epic count down and makes the spike outcome auditable.
