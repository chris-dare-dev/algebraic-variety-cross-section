# Anti-patterns

12 canonical anti-patterns with citations and rebuttals.  The orchestrator's `Anti-pattern guard` table in `commands/roadmap.md` surfaces the most common 10 inline; this file is the full set with sources for the user/auditor reading the skill.

| # | Tempting belief | Reality | What to do | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "Plan all of next quarter in detail before starting." | **Planning theatre** — production of artifacts that will not survive the first week of evidence. | Now in detail, Next in intent, Later as direction.  Rolling-wave detail decay. | Larson, *An Elegant Puzzle*, 2019, "Strategy" ch. |
| 2 | "Everything in MoSCoW is a Must." | **All-Must MoSCoW.**  Framework collapses; nothing is prioritized. | Cap Musts at <=60% of capacity.  `score-moscow.py` enforces.  If a "Must" doesn't fit, it's a Should. | DSDM Consortium *Agile Project Framework*, 2014, section 10.4 |
| 3 | "We need a 12-month roadmap to demonstrate seriousness." | **Locked long horizons** calcify into commitments and stop absorbing learning. | Anchor only the next 6-12 weeks; Later band stays directional. | Cagan, *Inspired*, 2nd ed., 2017, ch. 4 |
| 4 | "1 story point = 1 day, it's just easier." | **Story-point inflation** — points decouple from complexity, become disguised hours, lose comparability across stories. | Either commit to abstract points OR switch to T-shirts/#NoEstimates — don't conflate.  For solo work, use T-shirts and slice small. | Cohn, Mountain Goat blog, *"Why I don't use ideal days"*, 2014 |
| 5 | "Discovery is what PMs do; engineers just build." | **Missing discovery track.**  Without it, the team builds the wrong thing efficiently. | Maintain a parallel discovery track (Phase 1 evidence-gathering + spike lane in Phase 3). | Torres, *Continuous Discovery Habits*, 2021 |
| 6 | "Milestones and epics are basically the same thing." | **Conflating milestones with epics.**  Milestones are *date checkpoints*; epics are *bodies of work*.  Conflating them turns the roadmap into a delivery schedule and hides outcomes. | Track on separate axes.  The roadmap describes intent (epics); milestones describe commitments (per cycle). | Kniberg, *Lean from the Trenches*, 2011 |
| 7 | "We'll do `surfaces.py` first, then the panel wiring, then the tests — clean layering." | **Horizontal-only slicing** — nothing demoable until the last layer ships; the adversarial review (CONTEXT.md section 6 phase 3) has nothing to grade. | Vertical slicing always — every epic ships a user-observable change (a new dropdown entry, a new slider that visibly changes the render). | Holub, *Swim Lane Slicing*, OOPSLA-era + 2020 talk |
| 8 | "If we estimate well, we'll deliver well." | **Estimate-driven instead of outcome-driven.**  Optimizes for predictability of inputs, not value of outputs. | Outcome-driven planning: estimate only enough to schedule.  T-shirts on Initiatives, story-counting on Now lane. | Cagan, *Empowered*, 2020, ch. on "Roadmaps" |
| 9 | "The roadmap is what we've committed to ship." | **Roadmap-as-commitment** makes you defend stale plans against new evidence. | Roadmap is communication of intent; commitments live in the current cycle's epics only. | Bastow, ProductPlan blog, *"The Roadmap Is Not a Promise"*, 2018 |
| 10 | "A Gantt chart is just a precise roadmap." | **Gantt-chart-as-roadmap.**  Encodes false certainty about sequence and duration. | Now/Next/Later or outcome-rows; never Gantt. | Cagan, *Inspired*, 2nd ed., 2017, ch. 4 |
| 11 | "Discovery is slowing us down — let's drop it for more velocity." | **Dropping discovery for velocity.**  Velocity goes up, value-delivered goes down; you ship more wrong things faster. | Protect the discovery track even when it feels expensive.  Spike lane is non-negotiable in Phase 3. | Torres, *Continuous Discovery Habits*, 2021, intro |
| 12 | "We don't need acceptance criteria — the dev knows what to build." | **Tickets without acceptance criteria.**  "Done" becomes opinion; the adversarial review (CONTEXT.md section 6 phase 3) has nothing to grade against. | Every Now-lane story has Given/When/Then before implementation starts.  Validator enforces. | North, *Introducing BDD*, 2006 |

## Project-specific anti-patterns (algebraic-variety-cross-section)

In addition to the canonical 12, this codebase has caught these (each maps to one of the app invariants AI-1 .. AI-15 in `.claude/references/app-invariants.md`):

| # | Tempting belief | Reality | What to do | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 13 | "Let's add a Mayavi-backed alternative renderer for high-DPI users." | **AI-1 violation.**  Mayavi is broken on Apple Silicon as of 2025; matplotlib mpl_toolkits has painter's-algorithm artifacts; Plotly/k3d are Jupyter-first. | PySide6 + PyVista + pyvistaqt only. | CONTEXT.md section 3 + `.claude/references/app-invariants.md` AI-1 |
| 14 | "I'll add pytest-qt tests for the dropdown wiring." | **AI-2 / AI-3 violation.**  Qt+VTK segfaults under `QT_QPA_PLATFORM=offscreen` on macOS — CI can't run them.  Tests stay Qt-free. | Pure NumPy / PyVista / scikit-image tests only.  Render verification via `pv.OFF_SCREEN = True`. | CONTEXT.md section 10 + AI-2, AI-3 |
| 15 | "I'll use `clip_box(invert=True)` for the cube domain — it should work in 0.46+." | **AI-4 violation.**  `clip_box(invert=...)` semantics on PolyData are reversed/unreliable; commit `b68456f` documents the swap to scalar clipping. | `clip_scalar(scalars="_dist", value=r, invert=True)` for both sphere and cube modes. | CONTEXT.md section 8.2 + AI-4, AI-5 |
| 16 | "Run Taubin smoothing on the Hanson cross-sections for consistency with the implicit surfaces." | **AI-6 violation.**  Hanson parametric grids are already C^2; Taubin smears patch boundaries.  Implicit and parametric pipelines are deliberately distinct. | Implicit -> marching cubes + Taubin.  Parametric -> `_grid_to_polydata` + `_concat_polydata`, NO Taubin. | CONTEXT.md section 4.2 + AI-6 |
| 17 | "Use `compute_normals(consistent_normals=True)` everywhere for consistency." | **AI-7 regression** on Hanson cross-sections — 9-25 disconnected components can't be coherently oriented; produces per-patch lighting flips. | Hanson uses `cell_normals=True, consistent_normals=False, auto_orient_normals=False`. | CONTEXT.md sections 5.3 / 8.7 + AI-7 |
| 18 | "Add another `processEvents()` call in `appearance_panel` to keep the UI snappy." | **AI-9 violation.**  Without the `self._computing` re-entrancy guard, slider-release events re-enter `_render_current`, corrupting `_raw_mesh` and dangling actors. | Audit every `processEvents()` for the guard.  Centralize in `_render_current`. | CONTEXT.md section 4.4 + AI-9 |
| 19 | "Regenerate the mesh whenever any UI control changes." | **AI-10 regression.**  Domain-clip changes go from snappy (~ms) to laggy (~500ms).  The cached-raw-mesh + recompute-clip-only pattern is load-bearing. | `_on_domain_changed` calls `_apply_domain_and_render(reset_camera=False)` directly — no `surface.generate()`. | CONTEXT.md section 4.3 + AI-10 |
| 20 | "Use `Qt.AlignLeft` shorthand — it still works." | **AI-11 drift.**  Backward-compat aliases emit deprecation warnings; new code should use the qualified form. | `Qt.AlignmentFlag.AlignLeft`, `QSizePolicy.Policy.Expanding`. | CONTEXT.md section 8.9 + AI-11 |
| 21 | "Use `#888` for a muted text color." | **AI-12 / AI-13 violation.**  Short hex (`#888`) is rejected by PyVista's color parser; and the 3.5:1 contrast on small text fails WCAG AA. | 6-digit hex (`#5a5a5a` on `#f0f0f0` = 5.4:1).  Cite the contrast ratio. | CONTEXT.md section 8.3 + AI-12, AI-13 |
| 22 | "Call the new variety 'Barth's classical 65-nodal sextic' since the equation looks like it." | **AI-15 violation.**  Barth uses `(x^2+y^2+z^2)^2`, not `(r^2-1)^2`; the Endrass-normalized variant is a different surface.  Docstring honesty is load-bearing. | Cross-verify against >=2 sources (Wikipedia + MathWorld + arXiv + classical text).  Use honest "Endrass-normalized variant" / "real shadow" / "birational to" / "parametric cross-section" disclaimers. | CONTEXT.md section 5.2 + AI-15 |

## How to use this list during a Phase

- **Phase 1 (REFINE):** anti-patterns 1, 5, 8, 11, 22 are most relevant.  Re-read if you're tempted to skip Phase 1 ("the brief is clear") or to claim a new variety is "the genuine surface" without disclaimers.
- **Phase 2 (DECOMPOSE):** anti-patterns 6, 7, 12, 13, 16 are most relevant.  Re-read if you're tempted to slice horizontally, omit AC, or propose a non-PyVista renderer.
- **Phase 3 (SEQUENCE):** anti-patterns 2, 3, 4, 9, 10 are most relevant.  Re-read if you're tempted to lift the Must cap, lock long horizons, or flatten estimation.
- **Phase 4 (MATERIALIZE):** anti-pattern 12 (no AC) is the validator's main check.  Anti-pattern 9 is the framing reminder when offering the CONTEXT.md section 6 handoff.
- **AVC-specific:** anti-patterns 13-22 each map to a single AI-N.  Any candidate that proposes them is an AI-N conflict; the Challenger in `/capability-scout` and `/frontend-uplift` flags these explicitly.
